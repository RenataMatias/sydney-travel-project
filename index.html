Why Sydney?

Sydney, the vibrant city

A bright, modern city embedded in nature, where gallery-hopping, surfing, and fine-dining can all take place in a single day.

Sydney offers an urban mix of rich history and contemporary buzz, but with a distinctly Australian spirit. To understand why this city is so singular, take the clifftop coastal walk from Bondi to Coogee, where you will pass by beaches, rock pools, and lush parks. Then there’s the marvellous Sydney Opera House—jutting up like a great origami sailboat—anchoring the city’s iconic harbour. It’s also worth seeing the city from the top down; the views from the Sydney Tower Eye observatory are epic—and it’ll get you oriented on how everything is connected.

Places to visit

My favorite places in Sydney

Bondi Beach

Bondi Beach is an iconic stretch of fine sand and curling waves and one of the world’s most famous beach destinations.

What I like about it

The beach is well connected to the city using Bus and Rail. The views on the way are amazing. The walkway has all kinds of restaurants and shops on the streets.

Sydney Opera House

One of the most iconic buildings in the world – the Sydney Opera House is an architectural masterpiece and vibrant performance space.

What I like about it

It's a place where the past shapes the future, where conventions are challenged and cultures are celebrated. Sydney Opera House has something for everyone.

Darling Harbour

Scenic entertainment complex that features shops, restaurants, museums and other tourist attractions.

What I like about it

Darling Harbour is a must for any visitor. I have visited this place every time I have been in Sydney and the dynamics of things to do change all the time. Lots of events happen in the harbour with fireworks.
